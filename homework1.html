Beef In Creamy Mushroom Sauce

Make dinner extra delicious with fork-tender beef, and delectable mushroom gravy served over
steamed rice, mashed potatoes, or egg noodles!

This Beef in Creamy Mushroom Sauce is easy to make and cooks in one pan!

Main Ingredients for Beef In Creamy Mushroom Sauce

  • Beef - The recipe calls for sirloin which you can cut into thin slices, strips, or cubes

  • Mushrooms - I used canned whole mushrooms, but feel free to swap with fresh button or cremini mushrooms.
    Just slice and cook a little longer during the first step of the recipe.

  • All-Purpose Cream - you can substitute with heavy cream, evaporated milk, or sour cream.

Other Ingredients for Beef in Creamy Mushroom Sauce

  • 3 pounds beef sirloin
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • canola oil
  • 1 can (8 ounces) whole mushrooms, drained
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 onion, peeled and chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, peeled and minced
  • 1/4 cup flour
  • 3 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup table cream

Instructions

  1. Cut the beef across the grain into 1/4-inch thick slices.
  2. In a bowl, season beef with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. In a wide pan over medium-high heat, heat about 1 tablespoon oil. Add mushrooms and cook for about 30 seconds. Remove from pan and keep warm.
  4. Add another tablespoon of oil to the pan if needed. Add beef in a single layer and sear for about 2 to 3 minutes per side or until lightly browned. Do not overcrowd pan, cook beef in batches as needed. Remove meat from the pan and keep warm.
  5. In the pan, add the butter.
  6. When melted, add onions and garlic and cook until softened.
  7. Add flour and continue to cook, stirring regularly, for about 1 to 2 minutes.
  8. Gradually add broth, whisking regularly to prevent lumps.
  9. Add beef. Bring to a boil, skimming scum that floats on top. Lower heat, cover, and simmer for about 1 to 1 1/2 hours or until meat is fork-tender. Add more broth or water in 1/2 cup increments if the liquid is getting too thick before the meat is fully tender.
  10. Add all-purpose cream and stir to distribute. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  11. Add mushrooms. Continue to cook for another 1 to 2 minutes or until mushrooms are heated through. Serve hot.

Notes

  • For a more tender chew, slice the beef across the grain. You can freeze the meat for about 20 minutes or until partially firm to make slicing easier.
  • For extra depth of flavor, use condensed cream of mushroom soup. Reconstitute a 10-ounce can with the beef broth and omit the cream.
